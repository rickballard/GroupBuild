Funders & sponsors

What happens in a GroupBuild circle

1. Clarify your pivot

You arrive with a fuzzy worry ("I think my job is at risk") and leave the first sessions with a clearer direction to explore.

2. Explore options with AI

Together, you and your circle use AI tools to map skills, search adjacent roles, and surface realistic stepping stones.

3. Commit to small experiments

Each week you choose one or two concrete experiments. The circle keeps you honest and celebrates progress.

4. Track your journey

Light-weight notes and check-ins help you see how far you have come. You can keep this private or share parts of it.

Why circles, not just tools

Most people do not need another app or article. They need peers who show up regularly.

Circles are small enough for trust and large enough for diverse perspectives. AI tools become easier to use well when you see how others are using them, not just when you experiment alone.

GroupBuild circles combine structure, tools, and mutual support into something people can stick with.

How participation works

GroupBuild will start with small pilot circles.

  • Sliding-scale contributions for individuals.
  • Sponsored seats funded by employers, unions, or foundations.
  • No one turned away from early pilots for lack of funds.

We are in an exploration phase: testing what works, learning from participants, and sharing patterns others can reuse.

Who is behind GroupBuild

GroupBuild was started by people who have spent years building companies, working with organizations, and thinking about how AI reshapes work and civic life.

We are not a hype-driven startup promising overnight transformation and we are not a political party. We are a small team experimenting with ways to help people move through this transition with more dignity and agency.
