# GroupBuild Risk Register v1

This is a starting list of risks and mitigations.

## 1. Overpromising outcomes

- Risk: Participants expect guaranteed jobs or income.
- Mitigation:
  - Be explicit that GroupBuild supports process, not outcomes.
  - Use careful, honest language in all public materials.


## 2. Psychological harm

- Risk: Conversations surface anxiety, trauma, or despair that circles are not equipped to handle.
- Mitigation:
  - Provide clear boundaries and crisis resources.
  - Encourage facilitators to refer participants to professional help when needed.
  - Allow participants to step back from the circle if necessary.


## 3. Misuse for harmful organizing

- Risk: Circles or the broader platform are used to plan harmful or abusive actions.
- Mitigation:
  - Clear terms of use and moderation guidelines.
  - Active monitoring of public spaces.
  - Ability to remove participants who violate norms.


## 4. Data and privacy

- Risk: Sensitive stories or data leak or are misused.
- Mitigation:
  - Minimize data collection.
  - Use pseudonyms where appropriate.
  - Keep detailed stories within circles rather than central databases, where possible.


## 5. Facilitator burnout

- Risk: Hosts and facilitators are overburdened or under-supported.
- Mitigation:
  - Provide realistic expectations.
  - Offer support circles for hosts.
  - Design for sustainable workloads.


## 6. Brand confusion

- Risk: People mistake GroupBuild for a political party, a generic AI tool, or something else.
- Mitigation:
  - Maintain a clear public description.
  - Use FAQ and About pages to state what GroupBuild is and is not.
